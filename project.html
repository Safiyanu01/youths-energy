•Climate Action Hundreds of children are orphaned or abandoned and left hungry, homeless and without any opportunities for education. When child drop out of school, it becomes more difficult to reenter the school system. Out of school children are often forced to stay home and take up domestic chores, which exposes them to greater threats of domestic violence. However, when girls have access to education, they escape early pregnancy and marriage and are more likely to continue their education to higher levels. This, in turn, provides girls with the opportunities to fulfill their highest potential and become self-reliant. We are constantly seeking donors to sponsor childrens or less to take a good impact in education, school supplies, food & Clean Water. CLIMATE CRISIS We have been planting and planning to plant more in northern Nigerian States of Kano,Kaduna, jigawa,yobe,katsina, sokoto to Nigeria has the highest deforestation rate in the world, with only .36% of its primary forest remaining. Scientists believe planting 1.2 trillion trees (globally) would neutralize the damage caused by carbon emissions in the last decade. Please sponsor a child and Save a Life.

•Health Care Mortality rates among children are high in many countries with many children as well as adults suffering and dying from heart disease, diabetes and cancer. There are only a handful of hospitals/medical centers in the country but the cost is exorbitant. The average family cannot afford the costs of medical care or surgery if needed. Save A Life assists in assisting with the medical expenses for heart surgeries, cancer treatments and other illnesses. Infra-Structure/Housing Many in these impoverished countries have many poor villages and communities. Large families live in small huts or dilapidated and unsafe houses. Currently, we are calling on Governments, NGO's to repair or build modest homes so that they can accommodate their families.

•Homeless Children Generations upon generations, there is no end to the sight of young children of school age roaming the streets in a quest for survival. As an age-old tradition, these kids are popularly called ‘Almajiri’ – children from poor homes usually sent to Islamic boarding schools. Formal education remains a far cry for thousands of these children. (Almajiri were abandoned by their parents at Islamic centers. Almajiri are only allowed to study the Qu’ran and learn no employable skills - the result is over 80% of the terrorist group Boko Haram is ex-Almajiri.)
